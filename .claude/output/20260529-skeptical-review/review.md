## Cross-chain consistency summary (check 1)

- **Stack drift (finding #1)** is the most glaring: `05-roadmap` is written in
  Python filenames against a Bun/TS design. High-confidence doc-rot.
- **Taxonomy / state names are consistent** across 01 §7 → 02 §2 → 04 §3
  (`inform/query/request/response/control`; states `queued/delivered/surfaced/
  consumed/accepted/responded/expired`). `04 models.ts` `State` adds nothing
  outside the 02 state machine. Good.
- **Tool names** consistent: `ipc_send/check/reply/accept/decline/cancel/list/
  await/history/register` appear identically in 02 §12, 04 §6. `ipc_await` (04
  §6) maps to 02 §7 "await (opt-in)". No tool drift found.
- **`delivered_via` enum** consistent (03 §6, 04 §3 `DeliveredVia`) — but see
  finding #7/#14 on whether one column suffices for broadcast/per-recipient.

## Notes on what is genuinely solid (to avoid crying wolf)

- The delivery-ladder degradation framing (push optional, hook always-on) is
  architecturally honest and 00 row 4 correctly records `--channels` absent.
- Unix-socket transport choice (03 §5) sensibly dodges the port-conflict class.
- Stateless-thin-client decision (D5, 03 §2.3) is correctly forced by the
  one-MCP-instance-per-session host fact (00 row 5).
- The SC1–SC7-as-regression invariant (05 cadence) is good discipline — the
  concern (#17) is only that some SCs can't be proven by `bun test` alone.
